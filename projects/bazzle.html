Bazzle

Bazzle is a AR puzzle game made with Unity where the player has to try to get a magic ball into an energy cloud, helping it gain magic powers. The magic ball will always move forward, so the player has to make sure it doesn't fall.

The game features 6 different levels of increasing difficulty where the player has a set amount of pieces he can place to help the magic ball reach it's destination without falling into the void.

This game was made with a group of 3, for the subject "Augmented Reality" of the Bachelor's Degree in Video Game Design and Development at the UPC.

I'm really happy of the final result, as it was a great experience learning about AR and the game turned out to be fun and challenging. I wish it didn't get stuck as a simple university project...

My Contribution

Ball

As a Gameplay Programmer, I implemented the ball behaviour. This includes the movementforward movement, bounce and turn around on collision with a wall, aswell as the different reactions to the pieces.

Pieces

I implemented the behaviour of all the different pieces of the game, as well as the spawning system through the position of the card target.

The different pieces consist of:

  • Platform: It allows the ball to continue its movement, and if placed as a wall, will make the ball turn around.
  • Jump Rune: Allows the ball to bounce up in the air making it very useful to jump pits and obstacles.
  • Fire Road: Gives the ball a temporary movement speed boost.
  • Portal: Makes the ball teleport between the two portals of the level.

Game Loop

Developed the game loop, which includes detecting the "death" of the ball and restarting the level to it's original state to play again. Aswell as creating the different managers needed to handle the loading of each level and the possiblity of different ball skins.
